PROGRAMMING

My interest in programming began at the age of 8 years old. I was very fascinated by computers, and decided I wanted to learn to make a pop up box appear and say whatever I wanted it to. This led to me checking out programming books from the library until I learned some Visual Basic Script and made my young heart excited to have written my first program. I quickly began studying HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and creating basic web pages on my computer. By age 10, I started learning PHP and using it on an XAMPP server I installed on my Windows XP desktop to experiment with interactive and dynamic web experiences. At age 12, I installed my first copy of Ubuntu Linux and never looked back at Windows. In the meantime, I started developing plugins for Minecraft: Pocket Edition servers written in PHP around age 13 and became a highly acclaimed developer in their communities. Around age 14 I enjoyed getting into C++ development with Arduinos. I have since furthered my self-grown development skills to professional levels, having designed several applications for private companies. At age 16, I took my first formal programming class, CpS 109, here at BJU, which I excelled at despite doubts from my professor and academic advisors due to my young age; I even commonly stayed after class to help the other students with their programs. I have since taken CpS 110, CpS 230, CpS 320, and CpS 335.

Here I am today in CpS 105. Despite trying to get Dr. Knisely to let me skip this class for the last 2 years, I'm actually glad I'm taking it now. I find From the Garden to the City to be very intriguing and thought provoking
